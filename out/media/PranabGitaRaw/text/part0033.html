[Translators' note: What follows in the original text is a poetic translation in Bengali of the above meditation, which is further translated to English here. The Bengali translation is in rhyme. The rhyming aspect is avoided in the English translation here because of the literal and non-interpretive approach of the translation carried out throughout the book.]

 

OM

 

Meditation on the Gita

 

A poetic translation [of the Sanskrit verses above]

 

OṂ For the sake of Partha [Arjuna], the Lord Narayana Himself revealed You [the Gita]

Placed within the Mahabharata by that sage of the Puranas, Vyasa

Showering the Nectar of Non-Duality, You, the Goddess of eighteen chapters

Destroyer of worldly delusion; O Mother Bhagavad Gita, be the light in my search -1-

 

To you, O Vyasadeva, O you of immense intelligence

O you with eyes like the petals of a fully blossomed lotus, to you I bow

That you, who with the container full of the oil of knowledge

Made a lamp that emanates endless light -2-

 

The One Whose neck is dressed with the flowers of the wish-fulfilling tree

Beautified with the cane He holds

That Lord milked the Nectar of the Gita

With the jnana-mudra I bow to that Krishna -3-

 

All Upanishads together are the cows [of wisdom]

Their milker is cowherd-boy Krishna

Partha [Arjuna] is the calf, and the brilliant ones are the drinkers

The Nectar of the Gita is the Supreme Milk for them -4-

 

The Lord who was born as the son of Vasudev3

The destroyer of Kangsa and Chanura

The One Who is the Supreme Bliss of Devaki [birth-mother of Sri Krishna]

I give my salutations to that Guru of the Entire World, Krishna -5-

 

[3 Translators' note: As mentioned earlier in the book, the birth-father of Sri Krishna's name is “Vasudev[a],” with the first “a” being short in pronunciation. This is distinguished from Sri Krishna Himself being known as “Vasudev[a]” where the first “a” is pronounced long, as in: “Vaasudev[a].”]

 

That [river], whose banks are Bhishma and Drona, the water – Jayadratha, the blue lotus being the king of Gandhar

Shalya – the crocodile, Kripa – the current, whose billow – the intense waves of Karna

Ashvatthama and Vikarna – the terrible alligators, the whirlpool of Duryodhana

That river of battle was crossed by the Pandavas, with the Only Helmsman being Keshava [Sri Krishna] -6-

 

The lotus-words from the pure lake of Paaraashara [Vyasa], the essence of the Gita is the beautiful fragrance emanating from there

Many stories – like stamens [of the lotus] – are known by understanding the spiritual words within [the Gita]

The bees of good persons joyously drink the Nectar of the Gita

That destroyer of the sins of the Kali Yuga, may it [the Gita] be the bestower of our highest good -7-

 

The One Who makes the mute able to speak, by His Grace

The One Who makes the lame able to cross the greatest mountains

That Madhava [Sri Krishna], Who is the Manifestation of Supreme Bliss

With full devotion, I give Him my salutations -8-

 

The One Who is ever-praised with the singing of hymns by Brahmaa, Varuna, Indra, and the Maruts

The One Who is praised by the chanters of the vedas such as Sama Veda, with the Padas, the Kramas and Upanishads

The yogis, absorbed in meditation, who see Him in their Awareness through their practice

Prostrations to That Reality Whose end is not known by the gods and demons -9-

 

[Translators' note: The following prayer is only in Bengali [although the last line is in Sanskrit] in the original without a Sanskrit counterpart.]

 

Prayer for Grace

 

OṂ Hari Narayana Sri Madhusudan [Sri Krishna]

The Salvation of the fallen, the Resting Place of Compassion

The destroyer of ill fate/calamities, O Savior from the Ocean of Samsara

Bestow Your Grace, O Lord, by allowing me to be at Your Feet

OṂ Tat Sat BrahmaNe namah OM
