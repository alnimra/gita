INVOCATION

 

(Bengali translation)*

 

[* Translators' note: The parentheses -”( )” - and the text within them are in the original.]

 

The One Who, even though He is the Source and Lord of all, being bound by Compassion, knowing (understanding)** all of the mysteries of the matters relating to the Self, had Himself written [this wisdom] through the words of Vyasadeva– I bow to that Totality, worshipped by the gods, and the Perfection of all that is; and I hereby take up the work of writing commentaries on the [Bhagavad] Gita from the perspective of yogashastra. - 1

 

[** Translators' note: The parentheses -”( )” - and the text within them are in the original.]

 

The One from Whom this guna-based world's movement of creation, sustenance and destruction has come about, the One Who – although One – has, through wondrous means, taken on different forms, the One Whom the yogis – by stopping the senses and going beyond the darkness within – witness Him in the Stillness as the Person – I, who am of the lowest level, prostrate to Him again and again. - 2

Oh Wisdom-filled Gita! You too please have mercy on me, and have me understand you. [O] Wisdom, having become nectar, may you also continue your dance on the tip of my tongue, so that I may be able to speak your blessed, incomparable, spiritual mysteries, and have even the ignorant bathe with the Sweetness of Devotion of the Self - in the Nectar of the Bliss of the Supreme. - 3

 

The One Who, simply from His Compassion, by the arrow-like Wisdom, opened my darkness-blinded eyes and revealed to me the Indivisible Sphere [Cosmos] filled with the movable and the immovable, and Its Essence, Brahman – I bow to That very Sri Sri Gurudev. - 4
