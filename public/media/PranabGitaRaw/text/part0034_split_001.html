[OUTLINE OF PRANAB GITA – SRIMADBHAGAVADGITA]

 

PRANAB GITA

 

Srimadbhagavadgita

 

1st group of six [chapters]

 

karmakanda

 

[matters of karma [action]]

 

(Chapters 1 – 6)

 

1st Chapter:     Because of being deluded by the power of Maya, the sadhak becomes despondent in the very beginning, when he/she takes up the task of destroying the self-oriented attachments and vanities [expressing themselves as] “me and mine.”

 

2nd Chapter:     [The sadhak], after understanding the difference between the Eternal and the temporal by guru's grace through Sankhya-jnana [knowledge through the path of Sankhya], he/she looks towards destroying the “I am the body” idea.

 

3rd Chapter:     [The sadhak] takes up the performance of karma [act[s]] to rid him/herself of likes, dislikes, lust, anger [etc.].

 

4th Chapter:     Apprehending the collective knowledge of Sankhya-jnana and Karma-yoga, [the sadhak] casts away the vanity of knowledge gathered in ignorance.

 

5th Chapter:     By practicing the sadhana of unifying “prana” and “apana,” [the sadhak] gains a purity of chitta (pure chitta) and destroys the idea of [his/her own] doership.

 

6th Chapter:     Attaining the state of steady settledness, [the sadhak] engages him/herself in the meditation of the Self.

 

 

2nd group of six [chapters]

 

Devotion

 

(Chapters 7 – 12)

 

7th Chapter:     As a result of meditation, the sadhak nears the goal of meditation and becomes knowledgeable about “jnana” [spiritual knowledge] and “vijnana” [application of spiritual knowledge].

8th Chapter:     [The sadhak] comes to know – in the form of “Tarak Brahman-yoga” - the path that one never has to repeat again.

 

9th Chapter:     Clearly seeing oneself's attachment to the pleasures of the world, [the sadhak] sits in the yogic posture of “Rajvidya-rajguhya.”

 

10th Chapter:     [The sadhak] comes to know all of the Glories of the Supreme Lord.

 

11th Chapter:     Immediately upon knowing the Glories, the mind/heart sees the Universal Form [of the Lord].

 

12th Chapter:     Upon seeing the Infinite Form of the Self, the sadhak intensely experiences revelatory devotion.

 

 

3rd group of six [chapters]

 

[Spiritual] Knowledge/Wisdom

 

(Chapters 13 – 18)

 

13th Chapter:     The confluence of karma [act] and jnana [knowledge] brings the attainment of “Atma-jnana” [Self-Knowledge] to the sadhak. The awareness of the differences between Prakriti and Purush, the field and the knower of the field, and knowledge and the known comes about.

 

14th Chapter:     Upon becoming aware of the differences/divisions between the three gunas, the sadhak receives directives on arriving at [the state] beyond the three gunas.

 

15th Chapter:     “Ahaṃ” or “I” is established by the invisible Brahman in the depth of consciousness. The Supreme Bliss as this “I” arises in consciousness as the Ultimate Person or the Supreme Person. The aspects of the perishable, Imperishable and the Supreme Person become known.

 

16th Chapter:     The sadhak comes to know about the differences in matters of divine and demonic things.

 

17th Chapter:     Coming to know the differences between the three types of reverence, and becoming certain of all dharmas, the sadhak becomes cautious.

 

18th Chapter:     The subject of “sannyas” [renunciation] and “tyaga” [casting off] – the sadhak abandons all dharmas and attaining the knowledge of “mAmekam sharaNaṃ” [surrender to Me alone], the sadhak becomes Liberated.

 

At the end, the Kriyavan comes to understand that the Srimadbhagavadgita is the only recourse/support in the practice of yoga.
